About this project

This educational website explores storytelling as an Indigenous approach to teaching and learning. In many Indigenous traditions, stories are not simply narratives or entertainment; they are ways of sharing knowledge, transmitting cultural values, and strengthening relationships between people, land, and community.

The goal of this project is to examine how storytelling can be understood as a pedagogical practice and how educators can approach storytelling in respectful and thoughtful ways within educational settings.

Writing guidance for authors: This page should explain the purpose of the project and the academic context for the website. It should briefly introduce why storytelling is important in Indigenous educational traditions and explain how the different sections of the site are organized.

Purpose of this website

The purpose of this website is to introduce key ideas related to storytelling as pedagogy and to explore how storytelling can support learning in educational environments.

  • Introduce storytelling as a way of knowing and learning.
  • Explore storytelling as a pedagogical approach.
  • Discuss respectful practices when engaging with Indigenous knowledge.
  • Provide examples of possible classroom applications.
  • Encourage reflection on the responsibilities of educators.

How this site is organized

  • Teaching Through Story – introduces storytelling as a learning tradition.
  • Storytelling as Pedagogy – explores educational ideas and theoretical perspectives.
  • Respectful Practice – discusses ethical and respectful engagement with Indigenous knowledge.
  • Applications – suggests classroom and early childhood learning activities.
  • Reflection – invites educators to reflect on their roles and responsibilities.
  • Resources & Bibliography – provides references and further learning materials.

Suggested references for authors

  • Archibald, J. (2008). Indigenous Storywork
  • Kirkness, V., & Barnhardt, R. The Four R's: Respect, Relevance, Reciprocity, Responsibility
  • Simpson, L. B. (2014). Land as Pedagogy
  • Cajete, G. (2000). Native Science: Natural Laws of Interdependence

These readings provide important context for understanding Indigenous approaches to education and storytelling.
