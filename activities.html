Learning activities (draft)

These activity ideas are designed for early years settings and can be adjusted later. They avoid claiming specific Nation teachings. When you revise, align with your course expectations and community guidance.

1) Listening circle + “What did you notice?”

  • Read a contemporary Indigenous-authored picture book (choose one you can cite).
  • Ask open prompts: “What did you notice?” “What questions do you have?”
  • Offer response choices: draw, act it out, build a scene with blocks, or retell with puppets.

2) Story map with land and relationships

  • Use a large sheet of paper and draw places from the story (forest, river, home, etc.).
  • Children add characters and relationships (who helps who? who learns?).
  • Connect to care: “How can we care for our place?”

3) Retelling with loose parts

  • Provide stones, sticks, fabric, animal figures, small boxes.
  • Children retell the story in their own way (no “correct” version).
  • Educator documents children’s words (learning story style).

Accessibility tip: Offer visuals, gestures, and multiple ways to participate (talking, pointing, drawing, movement).
