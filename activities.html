Classroom & Early Childhood Applications

Introduce how storytelling can be thoughtfully and respectfully integrated into classroom and early childhood learning environments. This page may focus on practical applications that connect storytelling pedagogy to everyday teaching practice.

Emphasize practical and developmentally appropriate ideas for educators. Applications may highlight relationship, listening, reflection, creativity, and community learning rather than treating stories as simple classroom tools.

Possible directions for this page

  • Describe examples of classroom or early childhood learning experiences connected to storytelling.
  • Consider how storytelling can support children’s curiosity, reflection, and discussion.
  • Explore ways to connect storytelling to community, relationships, and land-based learning.
  • Include ideas for inclusive and accessible participation, such as drawing, movement, retelling, or dramatic play.

Possible activity examples

  • Listening circles or storytelling gatherings that encourage attention, reflection, and discussion.
  • Story retelling through art, movement, music, or dramatic play.
  • Connecting stories to children’s lived experiences, family knowledge, and community life.
  • Using storytelling to support reflection and conversation rather than testing for one correct interpretation.

Important considerations

  • Avoid treating Indigenous stories as generic classroom materials.
  • Use publicly shared stories or resources intended for educational use.
  • Acknowledge the cultural origins of stories whenever possible.
  • Encourage respectful listening, patience, and reflection as part of the learning process.
