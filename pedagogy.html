Storytelling as pedagogy (draft)

Storytelling can be a teaching practice that centres relationships, listening, and responsibility. Rather than a “lesson with answers,” storytelling often invites learners to reflect, make meaning, and connect ideas to their own lives and community.

Draft focus: Replace these general statements with your course readings and Nation-specific sources as you revise.

Pedagogical values you can highlight

Listening and reflection

  • Slow down and attend to words, tone, and silence
  • Reflect without rushing to “test” comprehension
  • Invite children’s wonder and connections

Relationship and responsibility

  • Stories connect people to land, kinship, and community
  • Learning includes caring actions (not just knowledge)
  • Children learn through participation and belonging

In an early-years setting

  • Use predictable routines: welcome, story, reflective chat, closing.
  • Offer multiple ways to respond: drawing, movement, dramatic play, retelling with props.
  • Support language learners with visuals, gestures, and repeated phrases.
