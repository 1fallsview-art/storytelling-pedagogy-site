Resources

This page provides a selection of resources that support learning about Indigenous storytelling, pedagogy, and respectful practice in early childhood and educational contexts. These resources include theoretical frameworks, educator guides, and multimedia materials that offer different ways to engage with storytelling as a relational and meaningful approach to learning.

The resources below are intended to support educators, students, and community members in continuing their learning. They offer opportunities to deepen understanding, reflect on practice, and explore storytelling in ways that are thoughtful, respectful, and grounded in Indigenous perspectives.

Storywork and Theoretical Foundations

These resources provide foundational understandings of storytelling as pedagogy, including relational learning, holistic approaches, and the role of stories in Indigenous knowledge systems.

  • Indigenous Storywork – A framework developed by Jo-ann Archibald that emphasizes principles such as respect, responsibility, reciprocity, reverence, holism, interrelatedness, and synergy. This framework supports understanding storytelling as a holistic and relational approach to learning.
  • Braiding Sweetgrass (Robin Wall Kimmerer) – A widely recognized text that explores relationships between people, land, and knowledge through storytelling. This work highlights the importance of listening, reciprocity, and respect in learning from the natural world.

Resources for Educators

These resources are designed to support educators in integrating Indigenous perspectives into their teaching in respectful and meaningful ways.

  • Empowering the Spirit – empoweringthespirit.ca
    This resource supports learning about Indigenous education, reconciliation, and culturally responsive teaching practices. It includes materials that connect to Treaty education and the Truth and Reconciliation Commission Calls to Action.
  • Empowering the Spirit – Learning Guide
    A companion resource that provides structured guidance for educators, including lesson ideas, discussion prompts, and reflective activities related to Indigenous knowledge and perspectives.
  • FirstVoices – firstvoices.com
    A language and cultural resource platform that supports the revitalization of Indigenous languages. It highlights the strong connections between language, storytelling, and cultural knowledge.

Videos and Multimedia Resources

Multimedia resources provide additional ways to engage with storytelling, including listening to Indigenous voices, perspectives, and experiences. These resources support learning through observation, reflection, and multiple ways of knowing.

  • Indigenous Storytelling: Expanding Ways of Knowing and Learning
    A video resource that explores storytelling as a way of understanding knowledge, identity, and relationships in Indigenous contexts.
  • Jo-ann Archibald – Storywork and Indigenous Education
    Video presentations and talks that explain the principles of Storywork and how storytelling can be used in educational settings.
  • Robin Wall Kimmerer – Whispers of Sweetgrass and Moss
    A recorded talk that explores relationships with the land and the role of storytelling in understanding ecological and cultural connections.

Key Takeaway

These resources highlight that learning about storytelling is an ongoing process. Engaging with Indigenous perspectives requires time, reflection, and a commitment to respectful practice. By continuing to explore these resources, educators can deepen their understanding and support meaningful, relational learning experiences.
