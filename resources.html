Resources

This page provides a selection of resources that support learning about Indigenous storytelling, pedagogy, and respectful practice in early childhood and educational contexts. These resources include theoretical frameworks, educator guides, and multimedia materials that offer different ways to engage with storytelling as a relational and meaningful approach to learning.

The resources below are intended to support ongoing learning. They offer opportunities to deepen understanding, reflect on practice, and explore storytelling in ways that are thoughtful, respectful, and grounded in Indigenous perspectives.

Storywork and Foundational Resources

These resources provide foundational understandings of storytelling as pedagogy, including relational learning, holistic approaches, and the role of stories in Indigenous knowledge systems.

  • Indigenous Storywork (Archibald)
    A framework that highlights principles such as respect, responsibility, reciprocity, reverence, holism, interrelatedness, and synergy.
  • Braiding Sweetgrass – Robin Wall Kimmerer
    A text that explores relationships between people, land, and knowledge through storytelling, emphasizing listening, reciprocity, and respect.

Resources for Educators

  • Empowering the Spirit
    https://empoweringthespirit.ca/
    Supports learning about Indigenous education, reconciliation, Treaty education, and culturally responsive teaching.
  • Empowering the Spirit Learning Guide
    A companion resource that provides lesson ideas, reflection prompts, and guidance for educators.
  • FirstVoices
    https://www.firstvoices.com/
    A platform supporting Indigenous language revitalization and connections between language and storytelling.

Videos and Multimedia

  • Indigenous Storytelling: Expanding Ways of Knowing and Learning
    Explores storytelling as a way of understanding identity, knowledge, and relationships.
  • Jo-ann Archibald – Storywork Talks
    Presentations explaining how storytelling functions as pedagogy in educational contexts.
  • Robin Wall Kimmerer – Whispers of Sweetgrass and Moss
    A talk exploring relationships with the land and storytelling as a way of knowing.

Visual and Reflective Resources

Visual and multimedia resources can support learning by offering additional ways to engage with storytelling. These formats encourage listening, reflection, and interpretation, allowing learners to connect ideas across different modes.

Key Takeaway

Learning about storytelling is an ongoing process that requires reflection, care, and a commitment to respectful practice. These resources support deeper understanding and help educators create meaningful, relational learning experiences.
