Resources (draft guide)

This page should list useful resources that support the project, such as community organizations, educational websites, publisher pages, or public-facing materials related to Indigenous storytelling and teaching.

Writing guidance for authors: Keep this page practical and selective. Include only resources that are helpful, credible, and appropriate for readers who want to learn more. This page is different from the References page: it can include educational or community resources, not only scholarly sources.

What this page should include

  • Community organizations, cultural centres, or Indigenous-led educational resources.
  • Publisher pages or author pages for relevant Indigenous children’s books.
  • Educational frameworks, curriculum resources, or public teaching guides.
  • Selected websites that are appropriate for further learning.

Suggested resource categories

  • Indigenous-authored children’s books and publisher information.
  • Local Indigenous organizations and cultural centres.
  • Educational frameworks and public teaching resources.
  • Library guides, museums, or community learning websites.

Image sourcing reminder

Use your own photos, public domain images, or properly licensed images. Avoid copying images you do not have permission to use.

Editing tip

Put full scholarly citations on the References page. Use this page for public-facing resources and helpful links.
