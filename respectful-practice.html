Respectful practice (draft guidelines)

This page is a starting checklist. You should revise it with your course materials and local community guidance. Different Nations and communities have different protocols for stories and teachings.

Key idea: Some stories are public, some are seasonal, and some are sacred or community-specific. Not everything is appropriate for classroom use.

Planning questions

  • Where is this story from (Nation/community), and who has the right to tell it?
  • Is it appropriate for public sharing? Is it seasonal or sacred?
  • Do I have permission, or should I invite a community storyteller/Knowledge Keeper?
  • How will I avoid stereotypes and “one-size-fits-all” messages?
  • How will children learn respect (not just information)?

Low-risk approaches (good for a draft site)

  • Use contemporary Indigenous-authored picture books (with proper citations).
  • Focus on themes like listening, gratitude, caring for the land, and community—without claiming specific cultural teachings you do not hold.
  • Highlight authors/illustrators and discuss “who is telling the story” and why that matters.
