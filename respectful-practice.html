Respectful Practice

Introduce the importance of respectful, responsible, and community-aware engagement with Indigenous storytelling in educational settings. Emphasize that stories should not be treated simply as classroom materials, but as knowledge that is connected to particular peoples, places, relationships, and responsibilities.

Focus on how respectful practice involves cultural humility, contextual understanding, and ethical decision-making. This section can help readers consider how storytelling must be approached with care rather than generalized as a universal teaching tool.

Key ideas to highlight

Consider explaining that some stories are public, some are seasonal, and some are sacred or community-specific. Not every story is appropriate for classroom use or public sharing.

  • Explain that stories belong to particular Nations and communities.
  • Discuss how different communities may have different storytelling protocols.
  • Emphasize the importance of approaching stories with humility and responsibility.
  • Consider how learning about stories also involves learning about relationships, context, and cultural specificity.

Planning questions

  • Where is this story from, and which Nation or community is connected to it?
  • Who has the right to tell or share this story?
  • Is the story appropriate for public or classroom use?
  • Could the story be seasonal, sacred, or community-specific?
  • Would it be more appropriate to learn from or invite a community storyteller, Elder, or Knowledge Keeper?
  • How can educators avoid stereotypes, simplification, or taking stories out of context?

Low-risk approaches for educators

  • Use contemporary Indigenous-authored books that are intended for public audiences.
  • Highlight the author and illustrator to discuss who is telling the story and from what context.
  • Focus on themes such as listening, relationships, community, and caring for the land.
  • Acknowledge that stories come from specific cultural and historical contexts.

This section may also include local community guidance, region-specific protocols, or reflections on how educators can practice reciprocity and responsibility in their own contexts.
