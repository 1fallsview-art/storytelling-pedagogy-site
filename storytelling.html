Storytelling (draft overview)

In many Indigenous contexts, storytelling is more than entertainment. Stories can teach values, relationships, responsibilities, and ways of understanding the world. The meaning of a story can change depending on the time, place, listeners, and purpose.

What stories can carry

  • Teachings about relationships (people, land, more-than-human life)
  • Community memory and history
  • Language, humour, and identity
  • Ethics: how to live well with others

What to avoid (draft reminders)

  • Treating stories like “content” to take without context
  • Assuming one Nation’s stories represent all Nations
  • Using sacred/cultural stories without permission
  • Forcing children to “extract” morals instead of reflecting gently
